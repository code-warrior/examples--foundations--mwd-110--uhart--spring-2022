Working with Dashes and the Hyphen

The Hyphen

Use the hyphen with hyphenated last names. For example:

Jennifer Rodriguez-Ghali

The Figure Dash

Use the figure dash with phone numbers. For example:

(860) 768-5925

(860) 768‒5925

The En Dash

Use the en dash to show through relationships, such as Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM through 5:00 PM, January through May, August through December. Example:

Mon - Fri

Mon – Fri

9:00 AM - 5:00 PM

9:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Jan - May

Jan – May

Aug - Dec

Aug – Dec

The Em Dash

Use the em dash where you might use parens (parentheses). It’s also used to sign a name. And, finally, as an expressive/dramatic final clause. Examples:

Be yourself; everyone else is already taken.

— Oscar Wilde (maybe)

- Oscar Wilde (maybe)

Sugar gliders are super cute animals - And they’re awesome!

Sugar gliders are super cute animals — And they’re awesome!

Sugar gliders are super cute animals, and they’re awesome!

Sugar gliders are - super - cute animals, nd they’re awesome!

Sugar gliders are — super — cute animals, and they’re awesome!

Sugar gliders are (super) cute animals, and they’re awesome!
